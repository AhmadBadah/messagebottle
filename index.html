Hello, I'm Ahmad

As I write on this virtual piece of paper, I can hear the buzzing of the drones all over our heads. I’m not on a desert island, like the perfect castaway of a novel, and I am neither alone. A friend said to me that, sadly, life does not follow the rules of a good movie or novel. Instead of a desert island, I am in the most crowded place I can imagine, a refugee camp in the center of the Gaza Strip. I, Ahmad, am 26 years old, and other displaced are here with me: my wife, two of our three kids, and almost my whole family composed of mum and dad, my siblings and their families. This is not the first war on us. I have seen bombings since I was a kid. Since the war of 2021, we still mourn my brother-in-law. We, as Gazans, grow up knowing too well that a war can kill us or our darlings at any moment. But this war is for sure the most horrific. If you are reading this message, it means that my virtual bottle floated in the sea of the Internet and reached you. And now you know: this is a mayday. I know that you, alone, can’t stop a war, but you can help me and my beloved to survive. Read our whole story by clicking the “Help us” button. Navigate through the updates to know more. If you can, consider donating and sharing my message. You can follow some daily life on my Instagram account or by coming back to this page at times. For everything, we are grateful.

Ahmad Badah

Software engineer, father of three

WHY WE NEED HELP

Living in Gaza has never been easy, but before October we had managed to find our happiness. We had a normal life, which sounds so special nowadays. We could work, cultivate our friendships, have a walk on the beach, hang out with our family, and give to our kids and nephews all they needed, and a bit more. We had dreams and plans, and we never thought that our dream would turn out to be a survival one. We don’t know how long we can survive in this catastrophe, and if we are not killed by shelling, starvation will. As you may know, we depend on aids, but sadly the aids are not entering the Strip. Supplies (any kind of supplies) are not enough, and this raises the price of everything. Food, medicines, water, but also baby diapers, and even summer clothing for our children: without your help, we could not afford those expenses. And then, there is the future: what will we do when this will be over? We want to walk on our legs, but I understand that we have to ask and accept your help to get back on the path of our lives. Everything you could do for us will contribute to our future. Thank you
